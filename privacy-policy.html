This Privacy Policy explains what personal data Time for TV ("the app", "we", "us") collects, why, who it is shared with, and the rights you have under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Dutch implementing act (UAVG).

1. Who we are

Time for TV is developed and operated by an individual based in the Netherlands, trading under the name MingusSama. Time for TV is not operated by a registered company.

For the purposes of the GDPR, MingusSama is the data controller for the personal data described in this policy.

Contact: mingus755@gmail.com — this is the address to use for any privacy question or to exercise the rights described in Section 8.

2. The app is social by design — content is public

Time for TV's social features work like Letterboxd: your public profile, username, avatar, comments, ratings, watch history, and public lists are visible to any other user of the app by default, not just people you follow. This is a deliberate design choice, not a bug or a leak. If you don't want a piece of information to be public, don't post it, or use the app's blocking/reporting tools (Section 6) to manage who can see or interact with your content.

Your account password and email address are never made public.

3. What personal data we collect

Account & authentication

  • Email address and password — if you sign up with email/password. Your password is hashed and never visible to us in plain text; authentication is handled by our backend provider, Supabase.
  • Google account basics (name, email, profile photo) — only if you choose "Continue with Google". Google acts as your identity provider; we receive the basic profile fields Google shares to create your account.

Profile & content you create (public, see Section 2)

  • Username, display name, and avatar image you upload.
  • Comments, replies, and any photo or GIF you attach to them.
  • What shows, episodes, and movies you track, mark as watched, and when.
  • Ratings and reviews you leave.
  • Custom lists you create, and whether they're public.
  • Who you follow and who follows you.

Moderation data

  • Reports you submit (reason, optional note, and what was reported) and users you block. This data is used only to operate the moderation system described in Section 6 and is not shown to other users.

Optional third-party integrations

  • Trakt.tv — if you choose to connect your Trakt account, we exchange an authorization code with Trakt to sync your watch history and ratings both ways. Trakt is an independent data controller for its own service; see Trakt's privacy policy.
  • TV Time import — if you upload a personal data export you received from the (now-discontinued) TV Time app, that file is processed to rebuild your library in Time for TV. The file itself is not sent to us — it's read on your device and matched against public show/movie databases (Section 4) to find the right titles.

Technical & device data

  • A push-notification token, if you enable notifications, used only to deliver reminders you've asked for (e.g. new episodes airing).
  • Basic request metadata our infrastructure provider generates in the normal course of operating the service (e.g. IP address at the time of a request), for security and abuse prevention. We do not otherwise log or profile your device.

We do not currently use any third-party analytics or crash-reporting SDK. If that changes in the future, this policy will be updated first.

4. Who we share data with

We use a small number of infrastructure and metadata providers to operate the app. None of them are permitted to use your data for their own purposes beyond providing the service to us.

Supabase

Our backend: authentication, database, file storage (avatars), and server-side functions. Your account and content data is stored on Supabase's infrastructure, hosted in London, UK. Acts as our data processor.

The Movie Database (TMDB)

Supplies show, movie, and episode metadata, artwork, and cast information. Requests to TMDB are proxied through our own server so no account or identity information is sent to TMDB — only the title/id being looked up.

Klipy

Powers GIF search in the comment composer. Your search text is sent to Klipy to fetch results; no account or identity data is shared with Klipy.

Google

Acts as an OAuth identity provider only if you choose "Continue with Google". See Google's privacy policy.

Expo

Relays push notifications to Apple and Google's own push services using your device's push token, if notifications are enabled.

Trakt.tv

Only if you explicitly connect your Trakt account (Section 3).

We do not sell your personal data, and we do not share it with advertisers — the app carries no advertising or ad-tracking SDKs.

5. International data transfers

Your account and content data is stored in the United Kingdom (AWS London region), via Supabase. Transfers of personal data from the EU to the UK are covered by the European Commission's UK adequacy decision, meaning the UK is recognised as providing an equivalent level of protection to the GDPR — no additional safeguards are required for this transfer.

6. Content moderation

To keep the community usable, the app includes reporting and blocking tools:

  • Reporting a comment hides it from your own view and is recorded against that content. It does not notify the person you reported.
  • If the same piece of content receives reports from multiple different users, it is automatically removed for everyone and the author's account is internally flagged for review.
  • Blocking a user hides their content from you, ends any follow relationship between you in both directions, and prevents them from seeing your public content interactions with them going forward.

Processing this data is necessary for our legitimate interest in keeping the platform free of abuse (GDPR Art. 6(1)(f)).

7. How long we keep data

We keep your account and content data for as long as your account exists. If you delete your account (Section 8), your profile, content, ratings, lists, and uploaded files are deleted from our systems immediately, along with your authentication record. Report records tied to content that no longer exists are periodically cleared out.

Some information may be retained for a short period beyond deletion where we are legally required to (e.g. fraud/abuse records needed to respond to a legal request), limited to what's strictly necessary.

8. Your rights

Under the GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectify inaccurate data (most profile fields can be edited directly in the app).
  • Erase your data — you can delete your account and all associated content at any time from Profile → Settings → Delete Account. This immediately and permanently deletes your cloud data; there is no recovery period.
  • Restrict or object to certain processing.
  • Port your data to another service, in a structured, commonly-used format.
  • Withdraw consent at any time, for anything processed on the basis of consent (e.g. disconnecting Trakt, disabling notifications), without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

To exercise any right not available directly in the app, email mingus755@gmail.com. We will respond within one month, as required by Art. 12(3) GDPR.

9. Children's privacy

Time for TV is not directed at children and is not intended for anyone under 16 years old, the digital age of consent under Dutch law. We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under 16. If you believe a child has created an account, contact us and we will delete it.

10. Security

Data in transit is encrypted (TLS). Access to your data in our database is enforced through row-level security policies, so the app can only ever read or write data belonging to the signed-in account (plus what's intentionally public, per Section 2). Passwords are hashed by our authentication provider and are never visible to us in plain text. No method of storage or transmission is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

If this policy changes materially — for example, if we add a new third-party service or start using analytics — we will update the "last updated" date above and, where the change is significant, notify you in the app.
